# ASI Briefing — March 2026: The Race Accelerates, Governance Catches Up

## The Three-Way Race

The frontier AI landscape has consolidated into a clear three-way race. OpenAI's o3 reasoning models push chain-of-thought capabilities toward human-level problem solving across math, coding, and science. Anthropic, now valued at $60 billion, bets on safety-first development with Claude 4's advances in reasoning. Google DeepMind's Gemini 2.5 brings multimodal reasoning and agentic capabilities, claiming state-of-the-art across 50+ benchmarks. xAI's Grok 3 enters as a credible fourth contender with massive compute scaling.

The race is no longer about who builds the biggest model — it's about who builds the most *capable* one. Reasoning, agency, and tool use have replaced parameter count as the key differentiators.

## Governance: From Voluntary to Mandatory

The governance landscape has shifted decisively from voluntary commitments to enforcement:

**EU AI Act — First Enforcement Actions.** The European Commission has initiated enforcement against high-risk AI systems in healthcare and law enforcement that failed transparency and risk assessment requirements. This is no longer theoretical regulation — it's operational.

**Third AI Safety Summit.** Held in early March 2026, producing binding commitments from 35 nations on frontier AI testing protocols, mandatory red-teaming, and information sharing between AI safety institutes. The word "binding" matters — previous summits produced only voluntary pledges.

**Global Expansion.** Brazil, India, and the African Union have all published new frameworks for responsible AI deployment, tracked by the OECD's AI Policy Tracker across 70+ countries. AI governance is no longer a Western concern.

**Brookings Assessment.** The 2026 governance landscape is shaped by three forces: EU AI Act enforcement, US executive orders, and China's deepfake regulations. Mandatory risk assessments for high-risk AI systems are now the baseline expectation.

## Alignment: Real Progress, Open Questions

Alignment research has moved from theoretical to practical:

**Interpretability scales.** Anthropic's latest interpretability methods now cover 60%+ of model behavior in key domains through circuit-level analysis. Transformer Circuits has demonstrated mechanistic interpretability techniques that work on models with hundreds of billions of parameters. Understanding what models *do* is no longer limited to toy problems.

**Scalable oversight works.** DeepMind's debate-style protocols show promising results for humans supervising AI systems more capable than themselves. This directly addresses the core ASI challenge: how do you verify something smarter than you?

**AI-assisted alignment.** ARC's 2026 progress report contains a striking finding: current models can meaningfully assist in their own alignment research. This creates a positive feedback loop — as models get more capable, they become better tools for making models safe.

**OpenAI's superalignment.** Progress reported on weak-to-strong generalization, automated red-teaming, and constitutional AI methods. The superalignment team, despite internal turbulence, continues to produce results.

## What This Means for ASI Builders

**The timeline is compressing.** All three major labs explicitly frame their work in terms of superintelligence. OpenAI's page is titled "Superintelligence." Anthropic has a dedicated "Superintelligence Research" section. This isn't marketing — it's organizational intent.

**Safety is becoming a competitive advantage.** Anthropic's $60B valuation is partly driven by its safety-first positioning. Investors are betting that the lab that can demonstrate trustworthy superintelligence wins — not just the lab that gets there first.

**Governance is a hard constraint now.** Any ASI-track project must account for EU AI Act compliance, summit commitments, and emerging national frameworks. Building in a regulatory vacuum is no longer possible.

**Interpretability unlocks trust.** Understanding 60%+ of model behavior is a meaningful threshold — it means stakeholders can begin to verify alignment claims rather than taking them on faith.
