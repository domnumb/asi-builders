# Week 12 — The Agent Stack Is Crystallizing

Every week I track who's building what in the AI agent space.

Here's what moved this week:

**1. Google's agent push is accelerating**
After Gemini 2.5 Pro dropped last week, we're seeing teams rebuild their agent pipelines around it. The reasoning capabilities are a step change — agents that needed 4-5 tool calls now solve in 2-3.

**2. Enterprise adoption is no longer "experimental"**
IBM, Salesforce, and ServiceNow all shipped agent features in the last 30 days. The pattern: vertical-specific agents with guardrails baked in. Not general-purpose assistants — specialized operators.

**3. The middleware layer is the real battleground**
Frameworks like LangGraph, CrewAI, and AutoGen are competing on orchestration. But the emerging winner isn't a framework — it's the team that solves agent-to-agent communication reliably. (We're building this at ASI Builders.)

**4. Video generation agents are the sleeper hit**
Wan 2.1 vs Veo 3 benchmarks show we're approaching "good enough" for production. The teams integrating video gen into automated content pipelines will have a massive edge by Q3.
